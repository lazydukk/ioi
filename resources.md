# Competitive Programming Resources

This file contains a curated list of books and online resources to supplement your IOI preparation, taking you from beginner to expert.

## Must-Read Books

These books are highly recommended for building a strong theoretical and practical foundation.

### **For C++ Language Mastery**

1.  **C++ Primer (5th Edition)** by Stanley B. Lippman, Jos√©e Lajoie, and Barbara E. Moo
    *   **Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
    *   **Why:** A comprehensive and authoritative guide to modern C++. It's excellent for building a deep understanding of the language features you'll use in competitive programming.

2.  **The C++ Programming Language (4th Edition)** by Bjarne Stroustrup
    *   **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
    *   **Why:** Written by the creator of C++, this book provides deep insights into the language's design and philosophy. It's a great reference for mastering advanced concepts.

### **For Algorithms and Data Structures**

1.  **Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)** by Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, and Clifford Stein
    *   **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
    *   **Why:** Often called the "bible" of algorithms. It provides rigorous and detailed explanations of a vast range of algorithms and is an essential reference for any serious computer scientist.

2.  **Competitive Programmer's Handbook** by Antti Laaksonen
    *   **Level:** Beginner to Advanced
    *   **Why:** A modern, practical guide written specifically for competitive programming. It covers all the essential topics with concise explanations and C++ code examples. **(Highly Recommended - Available for free online)**

3.  **The Algorithm Design Manual** by Steven S. Skiena
    *   **Level:** Intermediate
    *   **Why:** Focuses on the practical application of algorithms. It's known for its "war stories" (real-world examples) and a comprehensive catalog of algorithmic problems.

## Essential Online Resources

These websites are indispensable for learning, practice, and community engagement.

### **Online Judges & Practice Platforms**

*   **Codeforces:** The most active platform for competitive programming contests. Essential for regular practice.
*   **CSES Problem Set:** A fantastic collection of high-quality problems organized by topic, perfect for structured learning.
*   **AtCoder:** A Japanese platform known for its high-quality, educational problems and regular contests.
*   **TopCoder:** One of the original competitive programming platforms, with a huge archive of problems.
*   **USACO Training Program:** A structured training resource that takes you from basic programming to advanced algorithmic challenges.

### **Tutorials & Algorithmic Knowledge**

*   **CP-Algorithms:** An excellent and comprehensive resource for a wide variety of algorithms and data structures, with clear explanations and code.
*   **GeeksforGeeks:** A vast repository of articles on data structures, algorithms, and C++ concepts. Great for quick lookups and introductory tutorials.
*   **USACO Guide:** A modern, detailed, and well-structured guide covering topics from Bronze to Platinum level.
*   **learncpp.com:** An excellent, in-depth tutorial website for learning modern C++. Perfect for building a strong language foundation.
*   **Codeforces Blogs:** A treasure trove of advanced techniques, tutorials, and editorials written by top competitive programmers. Use the search function to find articles on specific topics.

### **Video Courses & Lectures**

*   **MIT 6.006: Introduction to Algorithms (Fall 2011)** on YouTube: Classic lectures that provide a solid academic foundation for the topics covered in CLRS.
*   **William Fiset's Data Structures & Algorithms Series** on YouTube: A comprehensive collection of video tutorials covering a wide range of topics with clear explanations and animations.
